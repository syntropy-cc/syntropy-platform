# Tutorial: Complete a Learn Track

> **What you'll learn**: Work through a full Learn track — multiple fragments and courses — until you have a complete, portfolio-ready project.
>
> **Prerequisites**: Account created; [Onboarding to First Artifact](01-onboarding-first-artifact.md) recommended.
>
> **Time**: ~1–2 hours (depending on track length)

## Overview

A **Track** is a project-centered learning journey: a sequence of courses, each made of fragments. Every fragment follows Problem → Theory → Artifact. By the end of the track you will have built a real project and earned collectibles and portfolio entries.

## Before You Begin

- [ ] You are signed in and have completed at least one fragment (see [01 — Onboarding to First Artifact](01-onboarding-first-artifact.md))
- [ ] You have chosen a track (from onboarding or the Learn catalog)

## Step 1: Open your track and see the map

In **Learn**, open your active track. Use the track map or course list to see:

- Completed courses/fragments (marked done)
- The current course and next fragment
- Upcoming courses (visible but not yet unlocked, if fog-of-war is used)

**Result**: You know where you are and what comes next.

## Step 2: Complete the next fragment

Open the next fragment. For each one:

1. Read **Problem** — what need or task this addresses in the project.
2. Read **Theory** — the concepts or techniques.
3. Build the **Artifact** in the embedded IDE or editor, using the reference artifact if needed.
4. Publish or submit the artifact so the fragment is marked complete.

Repeat for every fragment in the current course.

**Result**: The course is complete; you may receive a collectible or badge for that course.

## Step 3: Finish all courses in the track

Continue fragment by fragment and course by course until every course in the track is complete. The track’s reference project is the “blueprint”; your learner project is what you are building.

**Result**: The track shows as complete; your learner project is a full artifact (or set of artifacts) in that domain.

## Step 4: Check your portfolio and collectibles

Open your **Portfolio**. You should see:

- Updated XP and level
- Skills or competencies derived from the track
- Collectibles for completed courses
- The completed track and project reflected in your activity

**Result**: You have a verifiable record of completing the track and building the project.

## What You've Learned

- You completed a full track by doing every fragment (Problem → Theory → Artifact).
- Your portfolio and collectibles updated automatically as you progressed.
- You built a complete learner project aligned with the track’s reference project.
