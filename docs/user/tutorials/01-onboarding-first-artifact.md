# Tutorial: Onboarding to First Artifact

> **What you'll learn**: Create an account, get oriented on the platform, and publish your first artifact from a Learn fragment.
>
> **Prerequisites**: None; you will create an account as part of this tutorial.
>
> **Time**: ~20 minutes

## Overview

This tutorial follows the new-user path: you land on the platform, create an account, get a recommended starting point (career/track), open your first fragment, and build and publish an artifact. By the end, your portfolio will show your first verifiable contribution.

## Before You Begin

- [ ] A modern browser and internet connection
- [ ] The Syntropy Platform is available (e.g. production or a provided demo URL)

## Step 1: Open the app and create an account

Open the app URL (e.g. `https://app.syntropy.cc` or the URL provided to you). You land on the **institutional home** (landing page). Click **Get Started** or **Sign up**.

Create an account with your email and a password, or use OAuth (e.g. GitHub or Google) if offered. Complete any email verification step.

**Result**: You are signed in and see the main navigation (Learn, Hub, Labs, Portfolio, etc.).

## Step 2: Complete onboarding (career discovery)

If the platform shows an onboarding or career-discovery flow, answer the questions about your interests and goals. The system will recommend a **Career** and **Track** as a starting point.

Select the recommended track (or choose one that fits you) and enter the track.

**Result**: You see the track’s structure: courses and fragments, often with a “fog of war” so you see the next steps clearly.

## Step 3: Open your first fragment

Click the first **Fragment** in the first course. Each fragment has three parts: **Problem**, **Theory**, and **Artifact**.

Read the Problem and Theory sections. The Artifact section describes what you will build (e.g. a small script, a configuration file, or a document).

**Result**: You understand what you need to build and are ready to use the IDE or editor.

## Step 4: Build the artifact

Use the embedded IDE or editor in the fragment view. Create the artifact as described (code, config, or text). You can refer to the reference artifact if one is shown.

When you are satisfied, use the **Publish** or **Submit** action for the fragment.

**Result**: The platform creates a DIP artifact, links it to the fragment, and records the event. Your portfolio updates (e.g. XP, first achievement).

## Step 5: Verify your result

Open your **Portfolio** (or profile). You should see:

- Your first artifact listed or referenced
- XP or progress for the fragment/track
- Any first-time achievement or badge

You can also open the fragment again and confirm it shows as completed with your published artifact.

## What You've Learned

- You created an account and signed in.
- You used career discovery (if available) to pick a track.
- You completed a fragment (Problem → Theory → Artifact) and published an artifact.
- Your portfolio was updated automatically from that action.
