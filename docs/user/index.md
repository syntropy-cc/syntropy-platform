# Syntropy Platform

Syntropy is a unified platform where learning, building, and researching are one continuous journey — with a single identity, a verifiable portfolio, and the Digital Institutions Protocol (DIP) for ownership and governance.

## What is Syntropy?

Syntropy connects three pillars — **Learn**, **Hub**, and **Labs** — on a shared foundation. You learn by building real projects (Learn), collaborate in digital institutions with transparent governance (Hub), and run the full research cycle from hypothesis to peer-reviewed publication (Labs). Every action is recorded; your portfolio builds itself from what the ecosystem records, not from self-reporting. Artifacts you create are cryptographically anchored so that authorship and contributions are verifiable.

The platform is designed for learners who want to learn by doing, contributors who want to apply their skills to real projects, researchers who want open and reproducible science, and creators who want to own and be recognized for what they produce.

## Key Features

- **Unified identity and portfolio** — One login for all pillars; your portfolio is generated automatically from events (artifacts published, contributions accepted, articles published).
- **Digital Institutions Protocol** — Create institutions with configurable governance, artifact contracts, and value distribution; all decisions are recorded in a legitimacy chain.
- **Project-first learning** — Tracks are built around reference projects; every fragment ends in something you build (Problem → Theory → Artifact).
- **Integrated IDE** — Code, write, and run experiments inside the platform without leaving your context.
- **REST API** — Integrate with the platform programmatically: artifacts, institutions, projects, Learn content, Hub issues and contributions, Labs articles and reviews, AI agent sessions, and more.
- **Open peer review (Labs)** — Publish articles with DOI; anyone can review with passage-linked comments; reputation filters visibility.
